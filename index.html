The German Culture and Traditions.

Germany has a population of higher than 80 million, therefore being the second most populated country in Europe.
It is said that 91.5% Germans live in Germany, while the second highest ethnic group is Turkish at 2.4%.
In the aspects of arts, they had many popular musicians that composed classical music, for instance, Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart, Ludwig van Beethovern or Richard Wagner. Furthermore, there are many modern and old architectural styles to view in their cities from a wide variety of time periods. Also, one could visit places, where famous German poets used to live, like the Goethe House in Frankfurt am Main.

The German Language.

German is mostly spoken in West Germany and is an official language in Switzerland and Austria. It is probably the sixth most spoken language in the world and is spoken by around 90 million people. Moreover, there are different dialects, for instance High German (Hochdeutsch) and Middle German. High German was spoken before Middle German was spoken in 1100AD. Middle German is the language that the Bible was translated in by Martin Luther. The modern high German dialects that descends from the old Middle German dialects, is mostly spoken in the central and southern areas of Germany, Austria and Switzerland. Other dialects are Low German, which is still spoken in some nothern areas, Alemannic dialect, which comes from High German and Yiddish, which was spoken by German Jews since the middle ages and also comes from High German.
